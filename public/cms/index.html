Essential Content Fields

Understanding each field and how to use them effectively

Title

Why it matters: Your title is the first thing readers see and is crucial for SEO. A compelling title can increase click-through rates by up to 500%.

Best practices:

  • Keep it between 50-60 characters for optimal SEO
  • Include your primary keyword naturally
  • Make it compelling and benefit-driven
  • Use numbers, questions, or power words when appropriate

Examples: "10 Proven Strategies to Boost Your Blog Traffic" or "How to Create Engaging Content That Converts"

Slug

Why it matters: The slug is part of your URL and affects both SEO and user experience. It's automatically generated from your title but can be customized.

Best practices:

  • Keep it short and descriptive
  • Use hyphens to separate words
  • Include your main keyword
  • Avoid stop words (the, and, or, but, etc.)

Example: "boost-blog-traffic-strategies" instead of "10-proven-strategies-to-boost-your-blog-traffic"

Content (Markdown)

Why it matters: This is your main content where you provide value to readers. Quality content keeps readers engaged and improves search rankings.

Best practices:

  • Start with a compelling introduction that hooks readers
  • Use headings (H2, H3) to structure your content
  • Write in short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
  • Include bullet points and numbered lists for readability
  • Add relevant images and examples
  • End with a clear call-to-action

Markdown tip: Use the toolbar above the editor for quick formatting!

Featured Image URL

Why it matters: Visual content increases engagement by 94%. Your featured image appears in social shares and can significantly impact click-through rates.

Best practices:

  • Use high-quality, relevant images (minimum 1200x630px)
  • Ensure the image relates to your content
  • Consider adding text overlay for social media
  • Use free resources like Unsplash, Pexels, or Pixabay

SEO Optimization

Optimize your content for search engines and better discoverability

Meta Description

Why it matters: This appears in search results below your title. A compelling meta description can improve your click-through rate from search results.

Best practices:

  • Keep it between 150-160 characters
  • Include your primary keyword
  • Write a compelling summary that encourages clicks
  • Make it unique for each post

SEO Title

Why it matters: This is the title that appears in search results and browser tabs. It can be different from your main title to optimize for search.

Best practices:

  • Include your primary keyword at the beginning
  • Keep it under 60 characters
  • Make it descriptive and compelling
  • Include your brand name if space allows

Keywords

Why it matters: Keywords help search engines understand your content topic and match it with user searches.

Best practices:

  • Research keywords using tools like Google Keyword Planner
  • Focus on long-tail keywords (3-4 words)
  • Include 3-5 relevant keywords per post
  • Use keywords naturally in your content

Example: "content marketing, blog writing, SEO tips, digital marketing strategy"

Organization & Metadata

Properly categorize and tag your content for better organization

Categories

Why it matters: Categories help organize your content into broad topics, making it easier for readers to find related content.

Best practices:

  • Use 1-2 categories per post maximum
  • Create broad, evergreen categories
  • Keep category names simple and descriptive
  • Maintain consistency across your content

Examples: "Web Development, Digital Marketing, Business Tips"

Tags

Why it matters: Tags are more specific than categories and help readers discover related content on similar subtopics.

Best practices:

  • Use 5-10 relevant tags per post
  • Be specific and descriptive
  • Use existing tags when possible to create connections
  • Think about what readers might search for

Examples: "react, javascript, tutorial, beginners, frontend"

Author

Why it matters: Author attribution builds trust and authority. It helps readers connect with the person behind the content.

Best practices:

  • Use a consistent author name across all posts
  • Consider using your real name for personal branding
  • If multiple authors, maintain consistency in style

Status

Why it matters: Content status helps you manage your publishing workflow and content lifecycle.

Options:

  • Draft: Content is being worked on and not visible to readers
  • Published: Content is live and visible to your audience

Tip: Use draft status to save work in progress and review before publishing.

Content Success Strategies

Advanced tips to make your content stand out and perform better

Engagement Optimization

  • Write compelling introductions that hook readers immediately
  • Use storytelling techniques to make content more relatable
  • Include actionable tips and practical advice
  • Add relevant examples and case studies
  • End with questions to encourage comments

Readability Best Practices

  • Use short sentences and paragraphs
  • Include plenty of white space
  • Use bullet points and numbered lists
  • Add subheadings every 200-300 words
  • Write in a conversational tone

Content Planning

  • Research your audience's pain points and interests
  • Create content that solves specific problems
  • Plan content series to keep readers coming back
  • Update and refresh older content regularly
  • Analyze performance and double down on what works
